What's all this about rubber ducks? They are just inanimate objects, what could they possibly know?

That is precisely the point! They know nothing, so you must tell them everything.

When explaining issues or giving feedback, the best approach is to be clear, detailed, and patient. A brief guide

  • Start with Context: Begin by giving a brief overview of the situation. Explain what the problem is and why it matters.

  • Provide Background: Offer any relevant background information to help the person understand the context better. This could include past events, decisions, or actions that led to the current situation.

  • Facts and Details: Lay out all the facts, step by step. Be systematic and clear in your explanations. Assume the person knows nothing about the issue, so don't skip any important steps or details.

  • Use Simple Language: Avoid jargon, abbreviations and technical terms unless they're essential. Use simple language to ensure easy understanding.

  • Visual Aids: If applicable, use diagrams, screenshots, or other visual aids to illustrate the issue. This can make complex problems much easier to grasp.

  • Highlight Impact: Explain how the issue is affecting the overall situation or project. This helps the person understand the importance of resolving it.

  • Possible Solutions: If you have ideas for solutions or steps to take, share them clearly. Again, keep it simple and concise.

  • Patience: Just like you'd patiently explain a problem to a rubber duck, be patient with the person you're communicating with. They probably haven't experienced the issue first-hand, so work with them to resolve the issue.

  • Open to Questions: Be open to a conversation about the issue if clarification is needed.
